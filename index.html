A Little Bit Of My Story

Master student @ Peking University

Currently, I am doing research in National Drug Surveillance Center and School of Public Health, Peking University. Most of my work is related to the analysis data and model constructing of tuberculosis. I also spent part of my time on field survey of drug users.
 
My research mainly centered on the surveillance, treatment and prediction of tuberculosis, which has both the second largest number of cases and deaths among all infectious disease in China. For the surveillance part, we tracked the trend of resistance to four first-line and six second-line anti-TB drugs over the past 20 years in Zhejiang Province. As for the treatment part, we followed those multi-drug resistant tuberculosis patients for up to two years daily.
 
I also participated in many other research, working both independently and cooperatively. One project that I took great interest in is a study on drug addicts under compulsory detox. By using 40 Android-based tablets, we interviewed them about drugs they use, their mental and physical health status.
 
At the same time, devoted to make all my work reusable by other researchers, I published the research codes written in R, python and Matlab on Github (http://github.com/zhangle0127). Anticipating a more data-intensive research career, I practiced my programming skill through taking courses on Coursera.
 
I also contributed in translation work of a very classical book: The Population Biology of Tuberculosis. A brief introduction of the Chinese translated version can be found here: 结核病种群生物学.
